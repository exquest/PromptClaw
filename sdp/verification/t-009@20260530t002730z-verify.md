## Security

No shell injection paths found:

- Request-derived strings (`argv` elements) never appear in any `subprocess_run` `args` list — they are JSON-encoded and sent as `stdin`.
- Both local and remote subprocess calls pass `shell=False` unconditionally.
- NUL bytes are rejected before JSON serialization (local) and after JSON deserialization (remote).
- `remote_output_root` and `run_id` are validated for NUL and slash in `_remote_run_dir()`. The rsync `--` separator prevents a colon-prefixed or dash-prefixed run-dir from being misinterpreted.
- The `rsync` destination is derived from `host` (a constructor param, not request-derived) and the run-dir (validated above).
- No secrets, credentials, or host keys appear in tests or implementation.
- One observation: `run_id_factory` defaults to `uuid4().hex` (no slashes, no special chars). A caller-supplied factory that returns a path-traversal string could subvert `_remote_run_dir` — but this is documented as an internal hook, not a user-facing parameter, and the NUL+slash check at line 206 mitigates the most dangerous traversal characters. Acceptable.
