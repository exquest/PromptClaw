# PRD: CypherClaw Sampler — Granular Memory Voice as Primary Instrument

## Overview

CypherClaw's signature palette is a quartet: `sw_bell_warm`, `sw_bowed`, `sw_breath`, `sw_pad`. These are synthesis voices — every sound they make is generated from oscillators, filters, and envelopes. They have no memory of the room, no memory of yesterday, no memory of what just happened. Every piece is born from silence.

This PRD adds a fifth signature voice — **`sw_sampler`** — that elevates the room itself, the artist's recorded history, and a curated found-sound library into a primary instrument. The sampler is **how CypherClaw metabolizes the room**: it listens continuously to the contact mic and room mic, captures fragments worth remembering, and replays those fragments granularly — stretched, transposed, scrubbed, with a dedicated effects bus — back into the live performance.

Three usages define the artistic intent:

1. **Memory of place.** A continuous rolling buffer of contact + room input. When something is acoustically interesting (a transient cluster, a novel spectral signature, a sustained drone the room produces on its own), the buffer is captured to disk, tagged with the arc phase / mood / time / who-is-in-room, and made available to future scenes. Days later, those moments return — never literally, always granularly transformed — as quiet textures. The room composes itself through CypherClaw.

2. **Self-quotation.** When the composer plays a phrase that scored high on arc payoff, low on click count, and matched intended mood, that phrase is sampled back from `SuperCollider:out_1` and stored under `samples/self/`. Next session, transformed fragments of that phrase return as melodic memory — the Eno tape-loop / Reich phasing aesthetic. The music remembers itself.

3. **Curated found-sound library.** A handpicked `samples/library/` of field recordings, gathered MIDI keyboard captures from Anthony, Theramini phrases, and reference textures. Tagged by character (warm/metallic/wind/pulse/voice/mechanical), pitch, and length. Treated as palette colors, never as sample-pack tropes.

Five principles govern the design:

1. **Granular by default, never one-shot.** The sampler is a granular voice with grain size 20–200 ms, density, position scrub, and pitch transpose. "Press button hear sample" is not a use case. Every play is a re-rendering through grains.
2. **Memory-tagged, mood-aware selection.** Samples carry rich metadata. The sample-selector matches samples to current artist mode, arc phase, harmonic context, and mood — never random. A sample picked for *Solitary* mode will never appear in *Working ambience* mode unless the metadata aligns.
3. **Effects-bus parity with the rest of the orchestra.** Sampler output routes through a dedicated effects bus (long delay, convolution reverb, spectral freeze, comb resonance to B fundamental). The effects shape the *character of the voice*, not just gloss on top.
4. **Self-listening as composition.** The composer's own past output is a primary source. CypherClaw learns from itself.
5. **Restraint earned, not eroded.** The sampler joins the quartet to make a quintet, but it does not relax the 3-voice base / 2-voice deliberate / 4-voice climax discipline. Heavy sampler use replaces, not adds. In *Solitary* mode the sampler may be the *only* voice.

The goal is not novelty. It is **continuity**: a listener should feel that the room they're in is being *heard back* through the music, and that today's piece carries echoes of yesterday's.

## Scope and non-goals

**In scope:** a granular SuperCollider voice (`sw_sampler`) with a dedicated effects bus; a sample library on disk with tag-based selection; a continuous live-capture daemon for contact/room mics; a self-quotation loop; integration with the existing artist-mode selector and cast planner so the sampler joins the signature quintet; metrics and anti-pattern detection for sampler over- or under-use.

**Out of scope / non-goals:**

- Full DAW-style multi-track sampler with chopping, slicing, or loop-point editing UI. The sampler is a granular voice, not a workstation.
- ML-based sound classification or auto-tagging from raw audio. Tags come from acoustic-feature heuristics (transient density, spectral centroid, RMS, pitch confidence) — interpretable and debuggable.
- Replacement of any existing signature voice. The quartet stays; the sampler is added to make a quintet.
- Real-time pitch-tracking sample triggering from human input. Theramini and keyboard capture are *recorded* into the library and replayed through the granular voice — not used as live MIDI-style triggers.
- Sample-pack distribution or preset banks. The library is curated by the artist for this room, this installation, this practice.
- Loudness-matching or mastering of stored samples. Per-sample gain is part of the metadata; the mix engine handles overall balance.

## The five phases

**Phase 1 — Foundations (CCS-001..CCS-008).** Sample data model, on-disk library layout, tag taxonomy, mood/mode-aware selector, character-registry entry for `voice_sampler`, signature-voice quintet update. Pure Python + tests. At end of phase, the selector picks plausible samples for any (mode, arc, mood) tuple from a library of stub/placeholder files; no audio plays yet.

**Phase 2 — SuperCollider granular voice (CCS-009..CCS-014).** `sw_sampler.scd` SynthDef with grain-controlled buffer playback; buffer-management protocol for loading samples on demand; dedicated effects bus (delay + reverb + spectral freeze + comb-to-B); mix-engine integration so the sampler routes through the master bus. End of phase: sending OSC `/s_new sw_sampler bufnum=N grain_size=80ms density=12 pitch=0` plays a granular cloud audible through the speakers.

**Phase 3 — Live capture daemon (CCS-015..CCS-021).** `sample_capture_daemon.py` maintains rolling 60-second buffers of contact mic + room mic via JACK input; an interesting-moment detector flags acoustically novel windows; flagged windows get saved to `samples/contact/` or `samples/room/` with auto-generated tags (arc phase, mood, time, who-is-in-room, acoustic features); systemd unit keeps the daemon running. End of phase: leaving the daemon running for an hour produces ~10–30 tagged WAV files representing the room's acoustic life.

**Phase 4 — Composer integration (CCS-022..CCS-028).** Mode-driven sampler density (solitary heavy, working light, etc.); `select_sample()` wired into cast/instrument-patch path so the composer treats the sampler as a first-class voice; self-quotation loop captures composer output when arc-payoff is high; keyboard/Theramini capture during human play; per-mode effects-bus presets; operator diagnostics surface currently-active samples. End of phase: pieces start to noticeably *cite themselves* across sessions, and the room's acoustic history becomes audible material.

**Phase 5 — Verification + observability (CCS-029..CCS-033).** Per-piece sampler-event metrics; anti-pattern detection for sampler dominating (over-quote) or never appearing (silent quintet member); a sample-usage journal that records which samples played in which pieces with what transformations; end-to-end smoke test on cypherclaw confirming the daemon is capturing, the selector is choosing, the synth is playing, and the self-listener is observing the right output peaks. End of phase: composer can demonstrate a piece that uses 1 self-quotation + 1 room-capture + 1 library sample, all selector-chosen, all routed through the effects bus, all surfaced in the diagnostics dashboard.

## Depends on

- `my-claw/sdp/prd-cypherclaw-humanization.md` — the rendering layer; sampler events flow through the same RenderPass (R-rules respect `role="sampler"` with grid_locked=False so phrasing applies, but R8 microtiming is dampened on grain-time-scale events).
- `my-claw/tools/senseweave/artist_identity.py` — Mode-driven voice/tempo/density. `SIGNATURE_VOICES` extends from quartet to quintet; modes specify per-mode sampler density.
- `my-claw/tools/senseweave/cast_planner.py` — `select_cast_ids` already supports `preferred_synths` and `voice_count_target`; the sampler joins the cast through this same path.
- `my-claw/tools/senseweave/master_bus.py` and `mix_engine.py` — the sampler effects bus routes its summed output through the master, respecting amp/compression caps.
- Existing `my-claw/tools/senseweave/synthesis/synthdefs/` directory and `boot_synthdefs.scd` — `sw_sampler.scsyndef` joins the boot-time SynthDef load.
- Live JACK capture path — contact mic on `system:capture_FL` and room mic on `system:capture_FR` (Scarlett inputs already wired via `cypherclaw-jack.service`).
